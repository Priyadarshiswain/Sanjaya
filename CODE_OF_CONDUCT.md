# Code of conduct

Sanjaya is committed to a welcoming, respectful, and technically constructive
community.

## Expected behavior

- Treat people with respect, patience, and good faith.
- Critique ideas and code without attacking the person presenting them.
- Welcome questions and different levels of experience.
- Give credit for work and sources.
- Respect privacy, cultural context, and personal boundaries.
- Accept correction and repair harm when mistakes occur.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or sustained personal attacks
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- Publishing another person's private information without permission
- Deliberate disruption, intimidation, or bad-faith participation
- Retaliation against someone who raises a concern

## Scope and enforcement

This code applies in project repositories, discussions, reviews, events, and
other spaces where a person represents the project.

Before public participation is enabled, the repository will publish a private
conduct-reporting channel and identify the maintainers responsible for fair,
confidential enforcement. Public issues must not be used for sensitive conduct
reports.

Maintainers may remove content, restrict participation, or take other
proportionate action to protect the community. Enforcement decisions should
consider context, impact, patterns of behavior, and opportunities for repair.
